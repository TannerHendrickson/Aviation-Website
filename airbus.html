Airbus

Headquarters: Toulouse, France

Founded: 1970

CEO: Joerg Andres (as of 2024)

About Airbus

Airbus is a leading aircraft manufacturer and a global leader in the aerospace and defense industries. Headquartered in Toulouse, France, Airbus designs, manufactures, and delivers commercial aircraft, helicopters, and defense systems. With operations across multiple countries and a workforce of over 130,000 employees, Airbus is committed to sustainable aviation and cutting-edge innovation.

History

Airbus was founded in 1970 as a consortium of European aerospace companies to create a competitive alternative to American aircraft manufacturers. The company began with the development of the Airbus A300, the world's first wide-body twin-engine jetliner. From these humble beginnings, Airbus has grown to become one of the world's most successful aircraft manufacturers, revolutionizing commercial aviation through innovative design and engineering excellence.

Major Aircraft

  • A380: The world's largest passenger airliner, the A380 can accommodate over 800 passengers and is known for its impressive range and advanced cabin features.
  • A350 XWB: A modern wide-body aircraft featuring advanced composite materials, cutting-edge aerodynamics, and exceptional fuel efficiency.
  • A320: The world's best-selling commercial aircraft, the A320 family revolutionized short to medium-range aviation with its efficient design and passenger comfort.
  • A220: A modern, fuel-efficient aircraft designed for regional and short-haul flights with advanced technology and exceptional economics.

Business Segments

Airbus operates through several divisions: Commercial Aircraft, Helicopters, and Defence and Space. Each division is a leader in its respective market, providing innovative solutions to customers worldwide. Airbus is particularly known for its collaborative approach, leveraging expertise from across Europe and beyond to deliver superior products.

Innovation and Sustainability

Airbus is committed to sustainable aviation and developing next-generation aircraft with reduced environmental impact. The company is investing in hybrid-electric and hydrogen-powered aircraft to address climate change and meet future environmental regulations. Through innovation and collaboration, Airbus is shaping the future of aviation.

Global Presence

With manufacturing facilities and operations across Europe, Asia, and North America, Airbus serves customers in over 150 countries. The company employs approximately 130,000 people worldwide and maintains a strong commitment to supporting the global aviation industry and contributing to economic growth.
